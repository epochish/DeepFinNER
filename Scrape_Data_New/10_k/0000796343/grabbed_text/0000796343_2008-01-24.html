&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;WE ACCOUNT FOR STOCK-BASED COMPENSATION IN ACCORDANCE WITH SFAS&NBSP;123R. &NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;WE CURRENTLY USE THE BLACK-SCHOLES OPTION PRICING MODEL TO DETERMINE THE FAIR VALUE OF STOCK OPTIONS, RESTRICTED STOCK AND EMPLOYEE STOCK PURCHASE PLAN SHARES. &NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;WE ESTIMATE THE EXPECTED TERM OF OPTIONS GRANTED BY CALCULATING THE AVERAGE TERM FROM OUR HISTORICAL STOCK OPTION EXERCISE EXPERIENCE. &NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;LONG-TERM INVESTMENTS INCLUDE BOTH EQUITY HOLDINGS IN PUBLICLY TRADED COMPANIES AS WELL AS INVESTMENTS IN SEVERAL PRIVATELY-HELD COMPANIES. WE HAVE A POLICY IN PLACE TO REVIEW OUR LONG-TERM INVESTMENTS ON A REGULAR BASIS TO EVALUATE WHETHER OR NOT A SECURITY HAS EXPERIENCED AN OTHER-THAN-TEMPORARY DECLINE IN FAIR VALUE.

&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;&NBSP;OUR POLICY INCLUDES, BUT IS NOT LIMITED TO, REVIEWING EACH COMPANY'S CASH POSITION, FINANCING NEEDS, EARNINGS AND REVENUE OUTLOOK, OPERATIONAL PERFORMANCE, STOCK PRICE PERFORMANCE OVER THE PAST SIX MONTHS, LIQUIDITY, MANAGEMENT AND OWNERSHIP CHANGES AND COMPETITION. THE EVALUATION OF PRIVATELY-HELD COMPANIES IS BASED ON INFORMATION THAT WE REQUEST FROM THESE COMPANIES WHICH IS NOT SUBJECT TO THE SAME DISCLOSURE REGULATIONS AS U.S. PUBLICLY TRADED COMPANIES, AND AS SUCH, THE BASIS FOR THESE EVALUATIONS IS SUBJECT TO THE TIMING AND ACCURACY OF THE DATA RECEIVED FROM THESE COMPANIES. IF WE BELIEVE THE CARRYING VALUE OF OUR INVESTMENT IS IN EXCESS OF FAIR VALUE, IT IS OUR POLICY TO WRITE DOWN THESE LONG-TERM INVESTMENTS TO FAIR VALUE AND RECORD THE RELATED WRITE-DOWN IN OUR CONSOLIDATED STATEMENTS OF INCOME.
